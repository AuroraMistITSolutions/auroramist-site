FAQs

Your IVF questions, clearly answered

Trusted answers to the questions we hear most often.

The procedure is generally not painful, as it is performed under light sedation. Any discomfort is usually mild. We use a gentle anaesthetic given through an IV to keep you comfortable throughout.
Success depends on several factors — most importantly the patient's age, medical condition, history and the treatment approach chosen. Our specialists will discuss realistic expectations for your specific situation.
IVF may be recommended when other fertility treatments have not worked, or when it offers a higher chance of pregnancy than other options. In the absence of contraindications, it can also be chosen at the couple's request after consultation.
Common indications include blocked or damaged fallopian tubes, male-factor infertility (low sperm count or quality), endometriosis, age-related infertility, ovulation disorders that haven't responded to simpler treatment, and unexplained infertility. Your doctor will advise the most suitable path after evaluation.
In brief: the egg is retrieved under anaesthesia and fertilised with the partner's sperm in our lab. The resulting embryo is cultured for several days and then carefully transferred back into the uterus.
A cycle typically involves a few visits: an initial check-up and preparation, around two weeks for the IVF procedure itself, and then the embryo transfer. Our team will share a clear schedule tailored to you.
